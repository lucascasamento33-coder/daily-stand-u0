Bronx Gets $214 Million Senior Housing Development
The Bronx is about to see its largest transfer of affordable housing assistance on record. The Mamdani administration closed Wednesday on a $214 million development that'll bring 229 affordable senior housing units to the Morrisania neighborhood, with 80 of those units reserved specifically for residents of the nearby Morris Houses NYCHA complex. The project, called Sol on Park, will be built on the same site as Morris Houses in partnership between NYCHA, the city's Housing Development Corporation, and the Department of Housing Preservation and Development. Developers NRP Group and Selfhelp are leading the construction. It's the administration's largest project of its kind, coming as the city grapples with a persistent shortage of affordable housing for older New Yorkers. The move also marks a significant expansion of housing options for NYCHA residents, who've historically faced limited pathways to units outside the public housing system.
